Identity / Packaging

Maison Aure

A boutique perfumery identity built around silence, scent memory, and the feeling of unboxing something rare.

ClientMaison Aure
ScopeStrategy, identity, packaging
Timeline8 weeks

Challenge

Make a new fragrance house feel collected, not newly launched.

Maison Aure needed to enter a crowded luxury fragrance market without leaning on overused romance cues. The brand had to feel archival, tactile, and commercially clear across packaging, ecommerce, wholesale decks, and launch photography.

NoirNest created a restrained identity system with a wordmark, tonal palette, bottle labels, tissue pattern, sample cards, and art direction for the first collection campaign.

Signature Mark

A compressed serif wordmark and monogram system gave the bottles a collected, heirloom presence.

Material System

Soft black stock, blind debossing, and warm metallic accents kept the packaging quiet but memorable.

Launch Language

Short editorial copy created a sense of ritual without drifting into ornate fragrance cliche.
